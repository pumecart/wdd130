Being a work at home mom has its ups and downs. Yes, you have the option of rolling out of bed and going straight to "work" or rather your home office. Yes, you get to avoid hours of traffic and driving to work. You don't have to worry about packing a breakfast or a lunch because you can take advantage of your 24 hour kitchen whenever you feel hungry. There are so many pros to working from home. But wait a second, are you telling me that you also have a baby that you have care for? Well, that just changes EVERYTHING now doesn't it? There are so many things that others don't realize are going on in the background as a work-at-home mama with a newborn! Nothing like working to the tune of your newborn crying and possibly screaming because surprise, surprise! -- baby is hungry or wants to be held or needs a diaper change. Whatever it is, that baby needs your attention RIGHT NOW, and no customer or group meeting, or boss even, is going to change that. Not so easy typing out that report with one hand while the other arm is holding baby. Don't even get me started with sleep deprivation - because sleep is a distant memory -- sigh --. And yet there's sometimes an unspoken expectation to perform as good, if not better than before returning from your amazing maternity leave vacation because let's face it: you weren't working, so it was definitely a vacation! Does any of this resonate with you? Whatever you're going through, whatever you feel, join the BabyandIWork mamas community and vent, chat, CONNECT!
