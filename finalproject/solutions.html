Refresh, Reset, and Perhaps Buy!

Working from home with a newborn requires some creativity and even some out-of-the-box thinking. We've come up with some good ideas for how to recreate your workspace and have also taken into account that some of you mamas may be breastfeeding and some of you may be bottle feeding. Should any of these items be helpful, below the pics are links to the items mentioned at different price points: Save, Spend Some, and Splurge - to work with any budget! Enjoy!

Sit-Stand Desk and Baby Carrier

The sit-stand desk/baby carrier is a great combination. You can stand and keep baby close to you AND free your hands so that you can type and answer calls! You can even adjust the carrier so that you can breastfeed baby hands-free! Baby carriers come in a wide variety of prices with the cheapest options being to get one from a second hand store like "A Child's Place" where they always have a great selection of them to choose from. Sit-stand desks do run pricer starting from around $200 and up so we've included a cheaper alternative to add to the list: a desk converter (shown in the pic).

Baby Bottle and Hands-Free Bottle Carrier (and Breast Pump)

If you're bottle feeding baby, a hands free bottle carrier will be a life/hand-saver. Baby can drink and you can continue to work. ***This may be helpful for mamas who are breastfeeding also, but this will need to be paired with a good Breast Pump. You may find that breastfeeding isn't working out, but still want make sure your baby is getting your milk. You can pump during working hours and give baby the Hands Free Bottle Carrier to use. While your baby drinks you can use your scheduled breaks to pump!
