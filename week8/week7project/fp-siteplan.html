Site Name

BabyandIWork

Site Purpose with Goals

Imagine: You just had a baby. Congratulations! However, you're a working mom. You work from home and don't have anyone
else to help you take care of the baby during your working hours. Maybe you're breastfeeding. Maybe you have a job in
which you're constantly taking phone calls and can't have a screaming and crying baby in the background. What do you do? BabyandIWork.com is a one-stop idea shop on items that can be useful towards allowing a mom to work from home WITH a newborn.
From work desks to baby carriers, bottle holders and baby swings. We'll let you know what can help you and provide you with
links to buy them if you want. We'll also give you ideas of how you can set up your workspace to work for you and for baby.
You can also join our community of mothers and share your ideas that have helped you! Being a working mom is hard enough as it is.
With all the things you have to figure out, don't let working at home with your baby be one of them.
It is possible and BabyandIWork.com will show you how!

Target Audience

Our target audience is specific: Post-partum mothers who work from home, but it will serve anyone who works from
home with no one to watch their newborn. These people are looking for solutions to a fairly new problem, thanks to COVID.
People are working from home now more than ever and that is the new norm. However, most people and families don't
have anyone to care for their child while they are working. This site will identify important baby items that can help
soothe and occupy baby along with links for these items. It will provide ideas for arranging your work environemnt so that
it is easier to work and care for baby. It will also have a community page so that mothers and more can share their ideas,
give product reviews, and share their experiences with eachother.
There is no other site like it, so there is possibility for very heavy traffic. Other sites assume that you have the option
to create your own schedule. That is not true for all work from home jobs.

Refresh, Reset, and Perhaps Buy

Working from home with a newborn requires some creativity and even some out of the box thinking.
We've come up with some good ideas for how to recreate your workspace and have also taken into account
that some of you mamas may be breastfeeding and some of you may be bottle feeding.

Sit Stand Desk and Baby Carrier

The sit stand desk/baby carrier is a great combination. You can stand and keep baby close to you
AND free your hands so that you can type and answer calls! Baby carriers come in a wide variety of prices
with the cheapest options being to get one from a second hand store like "A Child's Place" where they always
have a great selection of them to choose from. Sit stand desks do run pricer starting from around $200 and up.
It's a bit of a splurge, but it's worth it ESPECIALLY if you're breastfeeding. You can adjust your baby carrier
straps so that you can breastfeed baby in the carrier, HANDS FREE! This is the ultimate solution for those mamas
who don't have a flexible work-at-home schedule. (Try to add a youtube

Baby Bottle and Hands Free Bottle Carrier

If you're bottle feeding baby, a hands free bottle carrier will be a life/hand-saver. Baby can drink and
can continue to work. ***This may also be helpful for mamas who are breastfeeding also, but this will need to be paired with a good
Breast Pump. You may find that breastfeeding isn't working out, but still want make sure your baby is getting
your milk. You can pump during working hours and give baby the Hands Free Bottle Carrier to use. While you
your scheduled breaks you can then pump!

Connect With Your People!

Being a work at home mom has its ups and downs. Yes, you have the option of rolling out of bed
and going straight to "work" or rather your home office. Yes, you get to avoid hours of traffic and
driving to work. You don't have to worry about packing a breakfast or a lunch because you can
take advantage of your 24 hour kitchen whenever you feel hungry. There are so many pros to
working from home. But wait a second, are you telling me that you also have a baby that you have
care for? Well, that just changes EVERYTHING now doesn't it? There are so many things that others don't realize are going on in the background as a work-at-home
mama with a newborn! Nothing like working to the tune of your newborn crying and possibly screaming
because surprise, surprise! -- baby is hungry or wants to be held or needs a diaper change. Whatever it is, that baby needs your attention RIGHT NOW, and now customer
or group meeting, or boss even, is going to change that. Not so easy typing out that report with one
hand while the other arm is holding baby. Don't even get me started with sleep deprivation - because
sleep is a distant memory -- sigh --. And yet there's sometimes an unspoken expectation to perform as
good, if not better than before returning from your amazing maternity leave vacation because let's face it:
you weren't working, so it was definitely a vacation! Does any of this resonate with you? Whatever you're going through, whatever you feel, join the
BabyandIWork mamas community and vent, chat, CONNECT!
